About Me

Hey, chat! I’m Amelia and this is my very modern portfolio. In case the blinkies didn’t make it clear, I’m trans, I drink tea, and I enjoy the internet aesthetics of the late 90s and early 2000s. Besides that, I also enjoy coffee, baking, listening to music, the ocean, brutalist architecture, vaporwave, clouds, playing video games, and obviously, making art. I started playing around with Blender during the summer of 2020, right after everything started shutting down. I started with simple tutorials, gradually adding my own flair to them until I had enough knowledge of the program to fully make my own art. Many of my early works were very clunky, much like an amateur artist who can’t shade, I too struggled with the basics (thankfully those pieces are forever banished to a dusty folder on my hard drive). However, I didn’t let that dissuade me, from chemistry lectures to 3 am discord calls, I was trying my hardest to get out of beginner hell. Eventually this coalesced in the following year when I decided that STEM didn’t really work with my brain and that I wanted to go to college for art; that, of course, landed me quite a few concerned looks from my family. After highschool, I ended up at a community college for two years where I took a few art classes specifically focused on digital 3D art. At that college, my technical knowledge improved substantially; I finally felt like I climbed out of beginner hell and could create whatever I set my mind to. I definitely consider myself a 3D artist first and foremost, but I also enjoy photography, drawing, and video editing.
