Mushling

At first these were thought to be predators, laying in wait in underground caverns hoping to get the drop on an unfortunate adventurer. Eventually some mushroom hugging druids explored the caverns and discovered these creatures are not infact predators, they don't even eat meat. Mushlings, as they are affectionately called due to their strange growth, live underground, scratching at the walls to expose roots and pull off moss. Their faces are structured similarly to spiders, in fact they have no relation at all, instead it is theorized to be an evolutionary trait to intimidate their would be predators along with the two large false eyes above their face. They have small mandibles for grasping onto broken bits of roots and loose moss which they then move into their mouths. Their backs are covered in hard plating and several spikes, due to the positioning of their face they are particularly vulnerable to ambushes and must have strong plating where they cannot see. Their growth has not determined use, some theorize their young rest in it, others believe it is for camoflagoue, in truth all assertions are valid. Mushlings live exlusively in humid caves with large lakes, seldomly venturing outside except in cases of great distress. Due to this, their eyes are hyper sensitive to sunlight, they struggle to see during the day while seeing perfectly at night, the few mushlings brought to the surface for domestication adopt a nocturnal sleeping pattern. Their meat is described as tough and gamey, they are primarily used in place of guard dogs, their look, size, and dolice demeanor result in them often beying left within sheep pens to scare away wolves during the night.
